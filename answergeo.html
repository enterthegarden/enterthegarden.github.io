Geography Knowledge Set

North America:
  • Northern & Western Hemispheres; Arctic Ocean (North), Atlantic Ocean (East), Caribbean Sea (Southeast), Pacific Ocean (South & West); 16.5% of land;
  • Large praires, towering mountain ranges, and varied coastlines; Rocky Mountains (West); Missouri & Mississipi rivers (Central USA), Great Lakes (North East); Tongass National Forest (Alaska); Chihuahuan Desert (South).
  • Pop. ~600M; 23 countries; Key countries: US (largest & most ppl), Canada, Mexico; Key cities: New York City, Los Angeles, Mexico City.
  • English, Spanish, French; History: settled in Last Glacial Period (~20k yrs ago), Norse exploration in 1000 AD, European colonization from 1492; Politics: mix of democracies, varying stability; Culture: Western traditions from European influence, indigenous pockets in Canada, US, Mexico, & Central America.

    South America:
  • Southern & Western Hemispheres; Caribbean Sea (North), Atlantic Ocean (East), Pacific Ocean (West); 12% of land;
  • Large grasslands, the towering Andes, and lush rainforests; Andes Mountains (West); the Amazon River (North), Lake Titicaca (Peru-Bolivia border); Amazon Rainforest (world's largest); Atacama Desert (North Chile).
  • Pop. ~450M; 12 countries; Key countries: Brazil (largest & most ppl), Argentina, Colombia; Key cities: São Paulo, Buenos Aires, Rio de Janeiro.
  • Spanish, Portuguese, indigenous languages; History: indigenous empires, European colonization, liberation; Politics: mostly volatile democracies; Culture: mix of indigenous, European, & African interactions, significant music contributions, strong passion for soccer.

    Europe:
  • Northern & Eastern Hemispheres; Arctic Ocean (North), Asia (East), Mideterranean Sea (South), Atlantic Ocean (West); 6.8% of land;
  • Fertile plains, rolling hills, and rugged coastlines; Alps (Centre) & Carpathians (East); Volga River (Russia) & Lake Ladoga (Russia); European part of Russian Taiga (North); Tabernas Desert (Spain).
  • Pop. 750M; ~50 countries; Key countries: UK, Germany, France, Russia (largest & most ppl); Key cities: London, Berlin, Paris.
  • English, French, German, Spanish, Russian; History: Ancient Greece & Rome, Middle Ages, Renaissance, World Wars, Cold War; Politics: democracies, monarchies + EU & Council of Europe for cooperation; Culture: Western civilization roots, diverse art, science, & history, strong emphasis on cultural heritage.

    Africa:
  • Northern Hemisphere; Mediterranean Sea (North), Suez Canal & the Red Sea (Northeast), Indian Ocean (Southeast), Atlantic Ocean (West); 20% of land;
  • Large deserts, rich savannas, and dense rainforests; Atlas Mountains (North-West); Nile River (North-East) & Lake Victoria (East); the Congo Rainforest (Centre); Sahara Desert (North).
  • Pop. 1.4B; ~54 countries; Key countries: Nigeria (most ppl), South Africa, Egypt; Key cities: Lagos, Cairo, Johannesburg; Largest country: Algeria.
  • Languages: diverse, incl. Swahili, Arabic, English, French, indigeneous; History: human origins, ancient civilizations like Egypt, European colonization, decolonization post-WWII; Politics: mix of democracies, monarchies, republics; cooperation by African Union; Culture: rich and diverse, significant art contributions, strong community emphasis.

    Asia:
  • Eastern Hemisphere; Arctic Ocean (North), Pacific Ocean (East), Indian Ocean (South), Euro & Africa (West); 30% of land;
  • Large plains, vast mountain ranges, and large rivers; Himalayas (South); Yangtze (China) & Ganges (India) rivers; Siberian Taiga (Russia); Gobi Desert (China & Mongolia).
  • ~50 countries; Pop. ~4.7B; Key countries: China (most ppl), India, Japan; Key cities: Beijing, Mumbai, Tokyo; Largest country: Russia.
  • Languages: diverse, incl. Mandarin, Hindi, Russian, Arabic, Bengali; History: cradles of ancient civilisations, birthplace of major religions; major recent decades of economic growth; Politics: mix of democracies, monarchies, & single-party states; Culture: diverse, ancient traditions to cutting-edge tech, strong family emphasis.

    Oceania:
  • Southern & Eastern Hemispheres; Indian Ocean (South & West), Pacific Ocean (East); 5.7% of land;
  • The arid outback, tropical rainforests, and the Great Barrier Reef; Great Dividing Range (East Australia); Murray River (Australia), Lake Eyre (Australia); Daintree Rainforest (North Queensland); Great Victoria Desert (Australia).
  • 14 countries; Pop. ~45M; Key countries: Australia (largest & most ppl), New Zealand, Papua New Guinea; Key cities: Sydney, Melbourne, Auckland.
  • English & indigenous languages; History: Settled 60k yrs ago, European exploration 16th c., WWII action; Politics: Democracies in Australia & New Zealand; Culture: rich indigenous traditions, Maori & Aboriginal heritage, strong ties to land & sea.
