Geography Knowledge Set

North America: Northern & Western Hemispheres; 16.5% of land; Rocky Mountains (West); Missouri & Mississipi rivers (Central USA), Great Lakes (North East); Tongass National Forest (Alaska); Chihuahuan Desert (South). 23 countries; Pop. ~600M; Key countries: US (largest & most ppl), Canada, Mexico; Key cities: New York City, Los Angeles, Mexico City. English, Spanish, French; History: settled in Last Glacial Period (~20k yrs ago), Norse exploration in 1000 AD, European colonization from 1492; Politics: mix of democracies, varying stability; Culture: Western traditions from European influence; indigenous pockets in Canada, US, Mexico, & Central America.

South America: Southern & Western Hemispheres; 12% of land; Andes Mountains (West); the Amazon River (North), Lake Titicaca (Peru-Bolivia border); Amazon Rainforest (world's largest); Atacama Desert (North Chile). 12 countries; Pop. ~450M; Key countries: Brazil (largest & most ppl), Argentina, Colombia; Key cities: São Paulo, Buenos Aires, Rio de Janeiro. Spanish, Portuguese, indigenous languages; History: indigenous empires, European colonization, liberation; Politics: mostly volatile democracies; Culture: mix of indigenous, European, & African interactions; significant music contributions, strong passion for soccer.

Europe: Northern & Eastern Hemispheres; 6.8% of land; Alps (Centre) & Carpathians (East); Volga River (Russia) & Lake Ladoga (Russia); European part of Russian Taiga (North); Tabernas Desert (Spain). ~50 countries; Pop. 750M; Key countries: UK, Germany, France, Russia (largest & most ppl); Key cities: London, Berlin, Paris. English, French, German, Spanish, Russian; History: Ancient Greece & Rome, Middle Ages, Renaissance, World Wars, Cold War; Politics: democracies, monarchies + EU & Council of Europe for cooperation; Culture: Western civilization roots, diverse art, science, & history, strong emphasis on cultural heritage.

Africa: Northern Hemisphere; 20% of land; Atlas Mountains (North-West); Nile River (North-East) & Lake Victoria (East); the Congo Rainforest (Centre); Sahara Desert (North). ~54 countries; Pop. 1.4B; Key countries: Nigeria (most ppl), South Africa, Egypt; Key cities: Lagos, Cairo, Johannesburg; Largest country: Algeria. Languages: diverse, incl. Swahili, Arabic, English, French, indigeneous; History: human origins, ancient civilizations like Egypt, European colonization, decolonization post-WWII; Politics: mix of democracies, monarchies, republics; cooperation by African Union; Culture: rich and diverse, significant art contributions, strong community emphasis.

Asia: Eastern Hemisphere; 30% of land; Himalayas (South); Yangtze (China) & Ganges (India) rivers; Siberian Taiga (Russia); Gobi Desert (China & Mongolia). ~50 countries; Pop. ~4.7B; Key countries: China (most ppl), India, Japan; Key cities: Beijing, Mumbai, Tokyo; Largest country: Russia. Languages: diverse, incl. Mandarin, Hindi, Russian, Arabic, Bengali; History: cradles of ancient civilisations, birthplace of major religions; major recent decades of economic growth; Politics: mix of democracies, monarchies, & single-party states; Culture: diverse, ancient traditions to cutting-edge tech, strong family emphasis.

Oceania: Southern & Eastern Hemispheres; 5.7% of land; Great Barrier Reef; Great Dividing Range (East Australia); Murray River (Australia), Lake Eyre (Australia); Daintree Rainforest (North Queensland); Great Victoria Desert (Australia). 14 countries; Pop. ~45M; Key countries: Australia (largest & most ppl), New Zealand, Papua New Guinea; Key cities: Sydney, Melbourne, Auckland. English & indigenous languages; History: Settled 60k yrs ago, European exploration 16th c., WWII action; Politics: Democracies in Australia & New Zealand; Culture: rich indigenous traditions, Maori & Aboriginal heritage, strong ties to land & sea.
